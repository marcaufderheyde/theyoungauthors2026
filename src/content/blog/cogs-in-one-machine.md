<p class="font_8">We see it all as the cogs of a machine,</p>
<p class="font_8">Everything that ticks away is something we’ve already seen,</p>
<p class="font_8">What someone’s done, where someone’s been,</p>
<p class="font_8">Those who radiate kindly beams,</p>
<p class="font_8">And those who strive to be the most mean,</p>
<p class="font_8">And those who just are and always will be,</p>
<p class="font_8">We are all the cogs, of the lonely machine.</p>
<p class="font_8"></p>
<p class="font_8">It has all happened, long before,</p>
<p class="font_8">We tend not to mention it, for danger of making our ego’s sore,</p>
<p class="font_8">For battling with our interests, and making life another bore,</p>
<p class="font_8">For all we want is more and more,</p>
<p class="font_8">Like the economic goals of a common red lit double d’s whore,</p>
<p class="font_8">More and more, of the same boring gore.</p>
<p class="font_8"></p>
<p class="font_8">We won’t learn, just because we can’t,</p>
<p class="font_8">We are a machine and not a developing plant.</p>
<p class="font_8"></p>
<p class="font_8"><em>by Datin Waters, 14, Notre-Dame International High School, Paris, France</em></p>
