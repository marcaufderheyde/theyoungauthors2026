<span style="font-weight: 400;">Paint dripped and dropped, splashing to the floors,</span>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">A woman cried alone in the street, as they came all at once,</span>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">Breaking down the poet’s doors.</span>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">The sun refused to rise that day, as it looked on down with a sorrowful gaze,</span>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">And the trees so young and filled with life, were hastily set ablaze.</span>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">The rumbling did thunder as the hammer fell down, again and again.</span>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">The children and their mothers clenched in agitation,</span>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">As they waited for their men.</span>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">And while all this was going on, Van Gogh did cut his ear,</span>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">For that which set these fires ablaze, was far too much for one alone to fear.</span>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">The birds made no sounds as the wind too ceased to whistle,</span>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">And too, the loud machines, which churned day on and night,</span>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">Halted to a standstill to discuss in silence the befallen plight.</span>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">For what all had seen, made all stand most still.</span>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">Those without a day been sick, suddenly fell ill.</span>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">The greatest crime had been committed, </span>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">The most heinous murder in the falling rain.</span>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">For this was the day, the most dreariest of days, that art,</span>

<span style="font-weight: 400;">Had been slain.</span>

&nbsp;

<em>by Marc Auf der Heyde</em>
