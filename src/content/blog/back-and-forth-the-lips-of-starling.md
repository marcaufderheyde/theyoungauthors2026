<p class="font_8">There’s one thing, I’ve picked it up,</p>
<p class="font_8">It’s the epiphany, my realization.</p>
<p class="font_8">Like the freedom birds can hope to enjoy,</p>
<p class="font_8">The sweet honeyed fruits of an endless nation.</p>
<p class="font_8">The feeling, of two rivers meeting,</p>
<p class="font_8">Two clouds becoming just as one,</p>
<p class="font_8">An endless carousel, a freakish circus,</p>
<p class="font_8">Being blinded by the sharps of the sun.</p>
<p class="font_8">The feeling of fresh tar laid down,</p>
<p class="font_8">Black and bitter to scorch your skin,</p>
<p class="font_8">You can’t resist to test what it would feel like,</p>
<p class="font_8">The feeling of being, the other twin.</p>
<p class="font_8">It pleasures as it does defeat,</p>
<p class="font_8">With every poison strand you take,</p>
<p class="font_8">You want it to last, you want it to stop,</p>
<p class="font_8">But whose heart will, end up in aches?</p>
<p class="font_8">by Marc auf der Heyde, 17, John F Kennedy School, Berlin, Germany</p>
