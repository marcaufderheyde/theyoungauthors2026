<pre class="wp-block-verse">How appetizing I must’ve seemed.<br>Ripe with life when I smiled and dreamed.<br>She plucked me from my garden patch<br>When the season called for a worthy catch<br><br><br>A hunger cares not for what it craves<br>It neither thinks nor hardly saves<br>A hunger waits with quiet might <br>Humored only upon sight’s delight<br><br><br>But how addicting her curation was.<br>For my queen I was happy to buzz.<br>With her needle’s thread she sewed us together<br>A stitch i thought would last forever.<br><br><br>Yet spinsters weave from wasted spindles<br>A thread that hurts and never dwindles.<br>Trapped in place by this newly stitched hem,<br>She ate my heart, then tied a knot from its cherry stem.<br><br><br>She showed me exactly how it’s done<br>Yet still I can’t do it on my own.<br>Through her movements I learned the game<br>But I still can’t say its entire name.<br><br><br>Weaving nets was something I could never master<br>But if I could, I’d also keep doing it faster<br></pre>
