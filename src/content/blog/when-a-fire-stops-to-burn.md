We all know <strong>what</strong> happens when a fire <strong>starts</strong> to burn,

A spontaneous combustion ensues and heat is given off with <strong>ease</strong>,

She wants to sugar coat the fire, make sure it can be put it out with a <strong>gentle</strong> touch,

But little does she know, <strong>she</strong> <strong>is burning</strong> the fire brighter than before,

"I'm still unsure", feeds my needs for O2's and C's.

&nbsp;

Because what happens is, when a fire stops to burn,

The world becomes a <strong>clearer</strong> place, the skies become much <strong>bluer</strong>,

I can think freely, <strong>unshackled</strong> presence,

But the fire is warm for her <strong>also</strong>,

Sugar coat, <strong>sugar rain</strong>, she feeds me ambrosia to feed the <strong>pain</strong>,

I'd rather take the pain, <strong>accept</strong> it for what it is now,

The fire will not stop burning for a while,

Plenty of time to send me your smile.
