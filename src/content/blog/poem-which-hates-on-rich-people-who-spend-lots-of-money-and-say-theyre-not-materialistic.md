<p class="font_8"><strong><span style="text-decoration: underline;">Poem which hates on rich people, who spend lots of money and say they’re not materialistic</span></strong></p>
<p class="font_8"></p>
<p class="font_8">Cubicle lives, like bees in a hive,</p>
<p class="font_8">There are some who deny the truth,</p>
<p class="font_8">And some who access it just to survive,</p>
<p class="font_8">Travelers and nomads,</p>
<p class="font_8">With their long dreaded hippy hair,</p>
<p class="font_8">They’re not so different, I’ll swear on it whole,</p>
<p class="font_8">For those are the ones who try to run from the lair,</p>
<p class="font_8">To run away from nature’s soul.</p>
<p class="font_8">They say time is their friend, but time agrees not,</p>
<p class="font_8">They lie in hammocks and smoke natural pot,</p>
<p class="font_8">“You just gotta chill, you’re just a follower of the system”,</p>
<p class="font_8">Well you don’t systemize when you’re high, you’re eyes just pop and seem to glisten,</p>
<p class="font_8">Am I a follower, because I have a job?</p>
<p class="font_8">Or are you just a washed up lost boy, who used to be an Eton snob?</p>
<p class="font_8"></p>
<p class="font_8"><em>by Paul Gracey, 17, Westerford High School, Cape Town, South Africa</em></p>
