<p class="font_8">Time - An uncontrollable fire</p>
<p class="font_8"></p>
<p class="font_8">31 million, and a little bit more,</p>
<p class="font_8">That’s how many seconds we have each year,</p>
<p class="font_8">And yet each year we wage the same wars,</p>
<p class="font_8">Open the very same doors and hope not to die,</p>
<p class="font_8">Do the same little chores and hope we can fly,</p>
<p class="font_8">Time will control us lest rivers run dry,</p>
<p class="font_8">But when they do that, our eyes will not cry,</p>
<p class="font_8">For nothing will move and nothing will be,</p>
<p class="font_8">Anymore, Time will grieve.</p>
<p class="font_8"></p>
<p class="font_8">I am so consumed, by what we call time,</p>
<p class="font_8">Give me just a little more and I will make this whole world mine,</p>
<p class="font_8">But money doesn’t affect those sands,</p>
<p class="font_8">Money works in euros and grands,</p>
<p class="font_8">But money affects us not, in age and wrinkly hands,</p>
<p class="font_8">That is something we never will control.</p>
<p class="font_8"></p>
<p class="font_8"><em>by Datin Waters, 14, Notre-Dame International High School, Paris, France</em></p>
