<p class="font_8">If my lungs could be clouds, I'd fill them with rain.</p>
<p class="font_8">And if your lips were fire, I think I'd suffocate.</p>
<p class="font_8">But all we are is flesh and blood and bones and skin pulled tight over our pretty souls and ugly chemicals strung together with such purpose.</p>
<p class="font_8"></p>
<p class="font_8">We are only reflections, mirror images of surrounding energy.</p>
<p class="font_8">Lonely and the same, lost in our glory and deceitful walls.</p>
<p class="font_8">We share our silent habits, breaths and time and no depth.</p>
<p class="font_8">How can two empty glasses be filled to the brim? Money is daunting; elitism terrifies us.</p>
<p class="font_8"></p>
<p class="font_8">All the same we indulge in our spoilt privileges, dancing on parallel lines.</p>
<p class="font_8">Maybe it’s your emptiness that draws me towards you.</p>
<p class="font_8">A blank canvas, same as me.</p>
<p class="font_8">The mere potential we possess unlocks daydreams and nightmares and unfelt feelings.</p>
<p class="font_8">I find fulfillment in the idea of your sympathy and intricate understanding petrifies me, despite my craving for layers of you.
</p>
<p class="font_8">Still, you remain unaware of the complexity that could be between us.</p>
<p class="font_8">If we crossed the shallow dividing stream, kinked our perfectly parallel lines, filled our hollow hearts and vacant souls with passion, love, hate, any emotion at all; we could create an energy, a world of our own.
</p>
<p class="font_8"><em>by Luna Korbey, 16, Nightingale- Bamford School, United States</em></p>
